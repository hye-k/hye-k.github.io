Time flies. I know it's such a cliché to say, but I mean it every time. 2025 is ending in literally two weeks, which means
'tis the time to write a year-end review.

## [Keep] Things I'm proud of

- deepening expertise in data engineering
  It was another year full of challenging projects, and I'm proud that I've successfully made it through them. Working in
  a small team, I've been in charge of a wide variety of projects autonomously. I'm not gonna lie, some were overwhelming.
  The more I work, the more I find engineering a very humbling job, as each and every project taught me lessons.
  One noticeable change I made this year is that I started appreciating macro perspectives more.
  I used to think better architecture, infrastructure, I've learnt a lot from my team mates so Huge thanks to my team!

- speaking at tech events
  I'd say it's been a memorable year in my career journey, as it's my first year to speak at tech events. In 2025, I spoke
  at 2 events, Women Who Code Korea and PyCon KR. I couldn't imagine myself being a speaker until last year, as I thought
  you must be a guru to be one. It turend out that was just my perfectionism deceiving. I submitted proposals for the events
  with little expectation to be selected, for the sake of trying. Thanks to the 'try anyway' mindset, I got the opportunities
  not only to share my perspective and story, but also to meet supportive audiences, some of which I became good friends.

- staying active outside of job
  participating in 15+ tech events
  launching 2 personal projects
  discovering new interests in NLP & AI Engineering

## [Problem] Things I wish to be different

- stress management
  Despite the fact that I enjoy my job, I must admit that data engineering can be quite stressful. As an IT engineer,
  I see my job is solving problems, and solving problems in data engineering feels like playing detective.

- procrastination
  There are some project ideas I've been planning to build, but I never got round them
